## Core Teaching

**Not this:** "You're not too old to learn to code."
**This:** Foundations enable adaptation. Patterns matter more than specifics.

---

## Story Arc Overview

This is a healing story disguised as a learning story. The visible arc is "started CS50 at 53, now working with AI at 56" but the invisible arc is "rebuilt identity after illness destroyed career by ritually returning to foundation-building."

**Emotional progression:** Wonder (childhood seed) → Gap (illness era) → Ritual return (CS50 as healing) → Flexibility (language-hopping enabled by patterns) → Gratitude (positioned for AI emergence)

## Tone & Voice Notes

### Overall Register
- **Not:** Inspirational. Motivational. "You can do it!"
- **Yes:** Matter-of-fact about struggle. Precise about process. Wondering about mystery.

### Key Tensions to Hold
- **Credibility vs. accessibility:** Has prior experience (credibility) but 30-year gap + illness (accessibility)
- **Age as advantage:** Not "despite being 53" but "grateful I started at 53" - maturity enabled foundation-first choice
- **Healing without confession:** Illness era is context, not subject. One sentence with weight.

### Load-Bearing Phrases
These carry meaning beyond their surface. Don't replace.
- "magical invocations"
- "calcify as we age"
- "reconnected me with who I was then"
- "create a new foundation"
- "patterns, the ways of thinking, the habits"

---

## What NOT to Include

- Specific CS50 curriculum details (problem sets, duck debugging, etc.)
- Step-by-step tutorial recommendations
- Extended illness narrative
- Defensive justification of CS50 choice
- Language wars (Python vs. TypeScript vs. Rust)
- AI hype or fear
